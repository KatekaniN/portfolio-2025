My Development Journey

From Numbers to Code

The personal story behind my transition from mathematics to software development

2018 - 2023
BSc Mathematical Statistics
Started my journey at University of Pretoria, falling in love with the logic and problem-solving nature of mathematics. I was fascinated by how numbers could tell stories and predict patterns.

What I learned

  • Analytical thinking and logical problem-solving
  • Working with complex datasets and statistical models
  • The beauty of finding patterns in chaos

The struggle was real

Financial constraints forced me to pause my studies with just 3 modules left. It felt like failure, but looking back, it was the push I needed to find my true calling. Sometimes the detour becomes the path.

curious determined challenged
2023 - Early 2024
Business Development Coordinator
Joined a US startup remotely, diving into the world of business strategy and client relationships. I was helping companies grow, but something was missing - I wanted to build the tools that made that growth possible.

Skills gained

  • Understanding user needs and business requirements
  • Cross-cultural communication and remote collaboration
  • Seeing technology from a business perspective

The turning point

Every day I worked with developers and saw how they brought ideas to life. I realized I didn't just want to talk about solutions - I wanted to build them. The seed of my coding journey was planted here.

ambitious restless inspired
Early 2024
The Leap of Faith
Applied to Umuzi's learnership program. I had no formal coding experience, but I knew deep down this was my path. The math background helped with logical thinking, but I was starting from zero in a completely new field.

Why this felt right

  • Coding felt like solving mathematical puzzles
  • The creative aspect of building something from nothing
  • The immediate feedback loop of seeing code come to life

Fear and excitement

I was terrified of being the least experienced person in the room, but also thrilled by the possibility of learning something completely new. Best decision I ever made - sometimes you have to trust your gut over your resume.

nervous hopeful brave
March 2024 - March 2025
Umuzi Learnership Journey
Intensive full-stack development training through Umuzi and Ogilvy. From writing my first "Hello World" to building complex applications, every day brought new challenges and victories.

Technical growth

  • Mastered JavaScript, React, Node.js, and PostgreSQL
  • Built real projects that solved actual problems
  • Learned the art of debugging and persistence
  • Won hackathons and gained recognition

The grind was worth it

There were nights I dreamed in JavaScript and weekends spent debugging. But seeing my Plant Care Assistant help people, or winning the FNB hackathon - those moments made every struggle worthwhile. I found my superpower.

focused growing proud
March 2025 - Present
Junior Developer at Ogilvy
Absorbed as a junior developer, now contributing to real client projects and mentoring the next cohort of learners. The boy who struggled with university finances is now building enterprise applications.

Current impact

  • Leading frontend development for major charity projects
  • Mentoring new developers and sharing knowledge
  • Contributing to enterprise-level applications
  • Building technology that makes a difference

Still growing

Every day I'm amazed that I get paid to solve problems and create solutions. The imposter syndrome is real, but so is the progress. I'm not the same person who left university - I'm stronger, more resilient, and finally doing what I love.

grateful confident purposeful
